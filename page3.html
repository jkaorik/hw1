Grilled Miso Chicken Recipe

A bowl of miso soup rights the wrongs. Lift the bowl to your lips, inhale briefly, take a sip of the deeply savoury liquor, and suddenly all is good with the world once more. It's the hot broth, of course â€“ hearty, fruity, virtually beefy in its intensity, perfectly clear when still then intriguingly cloudy once stirred, but there's more to it than that. The feel of the smooth lacquer bowl in your hands, hearing the occasional whisper that emanates from the tight, steam-freckled lid, and the smell, that is for me the essence of Japan, bring with them a serene pleasure.

You will need

  1. 4 tbsp of white miso
  2. 4 tbsp of mirin
  3. 4 tbsp of honey
  4. 2 chicken breasts
  5. 200g of bok cho

Directions

Put the miso in a mixing bowl, stir in the mirin, then add the honey and mix well. Score each of the chicken breasts three or four times, cutting through the skin and about halfway through the flesh. Place the breasts in the miso marinade, roll over to coat thoroughly, then leave in a cool place for an hour or so.

Put an overhead grill on low to medium. Line a baking sheet or grill tray with tin foil, add a couple of tablespoons of water to it, then place the marinaded chicken breasts on the foil with plenty of the marinade sticking to them. Grill the breasts for approximately 10 minutes, turning once or twice as they brown. Inevitably some of the marinade will burn, hence the importance of the foil.

Lift the chicken from its foil and serve it with grilled or steamed bok choi.
